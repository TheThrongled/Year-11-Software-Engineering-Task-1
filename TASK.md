# An exposition on not cheating.
ACADEMIC INTEGRITY
Academic Integrity refers to the expectation that all students within the All Saints community act with honesty, trust, fairness and respect when it comes to assessment. When a student acts without integrity, they are committing malpractice. Malpractice is any activity that allows a student to gain an unfair advantage. It is a serious offence that distorts legitimate measures of students’ achievements. There are four types of ‘Malpractice’ as defined by NESA:  breach of assessment conditions, misrepresentation, collusion and plagiarism. Further details of malpractice can be found in the College Diary. If misrepresentation, plagiarism or collusion are suspected, a student will be asked to prove that the work is their own. Any student(s) that has been found engaging in malpractice will be subject to the K-12 Assessment Policy which may result in a deduction of their assessment grade and/or receive a zero mark.
